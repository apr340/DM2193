From this article, it seems like the most important thing to consider when wireframing is to always remember who you're designing for. First, you need to make sure the entire team is on board and that everyone understands what needs to be done. Preparing for the problem will help things go much faster with less of a hassle. Other important things to remember once you've finished with the sketches is to understand the tools that you're working with. The project will surely go much faster as a whole if you know the ins and outs of what you're using, including all the various shortcuts to cut down on the overall time it takes to finish. You also need to remember who your audience is while working on the wireframe. You need to know exactly how much detail your audience will expect to see in your wireframe.
